Research Philosophy

Scientific stance and methodological principles guiding this research archive

Statement of Research Philosophy

My research philosophy is grounded in the understanding that neurodiversity represents a complex, heterogeneous, and dynamic dimension of human variation, rather than a set of isolated deficits or categorical pathologies.

I approach scientific research with the conviction that meaningful knowledge in neurodevelopmental and neurological conditions emerges from the careful integration of data, context, and ethical responsibility.

I view knowledge as provisional and cumulative. Scientific evidence is continuously refined as new data, methods, and perspectives become available. For this reason, I prioritize transparency, reproducibility, and methodological rigor, while remaining attentive to the limitations inherent in observational data, clinical samples, and statistical modeling.

Methodologically, my work integrates quantitative analysis, statistical reasoning, and bioinformatics with domain knowledge from neuroscience and mental health research. Data analysis is treated as a tool for understanding biological and functional patterns, not as an instrument for oversimplification.

Exploratory analyses, when conducted responsibly, are essential for generating hypotheses, revealing latent structures, and identifying meaningful associations that may not be apparent through purely confirmatory approaches.

Ethically, my research is guided by a commitment to non-reductionism and respect for individual variability. In the context of neurodiversity, I am particularly cautious about interpretations that frame neurological differences solely in terms of impairment, ignoring adaptive traits, environmental interactions, and lived experience.

I emphasize critical evaluation and curation of scientific literature, with attention to study design, sample characteristics, and analytical decisions. By organizing and synthesizing research across domains such as autism, ADHD, migraine, and psychiatric comorbidities, my aim is to contribute to a more integrated and nuanced understanding of neurodevelopmental conditions and their real-world impact.

Ultimately, I view research as both a technical and ethical endeavor. Sound analysis, clear methodology, and responsible interpretation are inseparable. My goal is to contribute to scientific knowledge that is robust, transparent, and respectful of human diversity.
