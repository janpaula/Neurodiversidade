This project is a curated collection of scientific articles focused on neurodiversity, neurodevelopmental conditions, their clinical, functional and societal implications.

The archive was created to organize peer-reviewed research across themes such as autism spectrum conditions, ADHD, migraine, and neurodevelopmental comorbidities, with the goal of facilitating interdisciplinary understanding and evidence-based discussion.

Background

I am a systems analyst with a strong interest in data analysis, bioinformatics, and the application of computational approaches to health and neuroscience.

This archive reflects an effort to bridge technical, clinical, and scientific perspectives, emphasizing accessibility, methodological rigor and clarity of information.

Scope and intent

The scientific version of this archive is intended for academic, educational and research-oriented audiences. Articles are grouped thematically and selected based on relevance, methodological quality and contribution to current scientific discourse.

This project is not intended to replace clinical guidance or medical advice, but rather to support informed reading and academic exploration.
